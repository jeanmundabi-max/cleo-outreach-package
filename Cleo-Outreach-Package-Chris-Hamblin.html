How Cleo Gets More Users and Converts Them

Your full funnel mapped. Each stage shows what happens now, what is missing, and what we would install. Specific to diversification products, not core EWA.

Top of Funnel: Getting Found

Now: Meta and TikTok for core EWA. Paid social drives volume. But diversification products (Credit Builder, Chat, UK) don't have proven channels.

Missing: Google Search for Credit Builder keywords ("how to build credit," "credit score app"). UK creator partnerships for relaunch. LinkedIn thought leadership for Chris's positioning. Product specific creative (one size fits all creative does not work across 4 product lines).

Install: Channel specific acquisition per product. Google Search for Credit Builder. TikTok discovery for Chat. UK creator partnerships. Each product gets its own channel strategy, not a shared campaign.

Middle of Funnel: Nurture and Educate

Now: Users download the app and hit a paywall. Core product (EWA) has a known conversion path. Diversification products do not.

Missing: Product specific onboarding. A Credit Builder user and an AI Chat user have different needs but see the same first screen. Time to value is unclear for new products. Post FTC messaging that builds trust through transparency.

Install: Product specific onboarding flows. "Here is exactly what you get for $5.99. No surprises." Reversed paywalls (value before price). FTC compliant templates that convert THROUGH transparency, not despite it.

Bottom of Funnel: Convert

Now: Subscription paywall. Core EWA converts because the value is immediate (cash advance = instant gratification).

Missing: Diversification products lack instant gratification. Credit Builder takes months to show results. Chat is ongoing value, not a single moment. UK users have no reference point for Cleo. Conversion paths need to match each product's value timeline.

Install: Product specific conversion triggers. Credit Builder: "your score improved by X points" milestone. Chat: "you saved X this month" milestone. UK: social proof from early adopters. Attribution separated by product so the board sees real unit economics per line.

Sale: Expand and Retain

Now: Users subscribe. Core product retains through recurring need (cash advances).

Missing: Cross-sell between products. A Credit Builder user who also needs budgeting should see Chat. Retention for products without recurring urgency. UK expansion revenue tracking.

Install: Cross-sell triggers based on usage patterns. Product bundling experiments. Retention sequences per product. UK cohort analysis separated from US.

Fix Top and Middle first. Diversification products need their own acquisition paths, not a copy of the EWA playbook.

90 Day Roadmap

Weeks 1 to 6: Test and Validate

Week 1

Infrastructure Setup

Create product specific landing pages: meetcleo.com/credit-builder, meetcleo.com/uk. Set up separated tracking and attribution per product. Build the "What brought you to Cleo?" onboarding branch.

Week 2

UK Conversion Page

Replace the UK waitlist with a full conversion experience. Hero section: "Your bank shows you what you spent. Cleo shows you what to do about it." Include UK beta testimonials and Monzo/Starling positioning.

Week 3

First Tests Launch

Credit Builder: Google Search campaigns targeting "build credit app," "improve credit score app." UK: Instagram and Reddit test campaigns. Chat: TikTok discovery format with screen recordings of real Cleo AI conversations.

Week 4

Go/No-Go Decision

Measure CPA, download to account, account to subscribe, and 7 day retention per product per channel. Credit Builder CPA under $60? UK CPA under GBP 35? Scale the winners. Kill the losers.

Week 5

Paywall Experiment

Test reversed paywall for Credit Builder (show credit score free, paywall at improvement plan) and Chat (3 free roast conversations, paywall at conversation 4). Compare subscription conversion against current flow.

Week 6

FTC Transparent Messaging Test

A/B test transparent pricing creative ("Here's exactly what you get for $5.99") against current approach. Measure trust signals: completion rate, review sentiment, cancellation rate at 30 days.

Weeks 7 to 12: Optimise and Scale

Week 7

Scale Winners

Products and channels that passed go/no-go get 3x budget increase. Launch 3 to 5 new creative angles per winning product. Kill underperformers without sentiment.

Week 8

App Store Custom Product Pages

Align Apple custom product pages with each campaign. Credit Builder screenshots for credit campaigns. UK features for UK campaigns. Stop sending diverse traffic to one generic listing.

Week 9

UK Creator Partnerships

Launch 2 to 3 YouTube UK fintech creator partnerships. Format: authentic "I tried Cleo for a week" reviews. Reddit r/UKPersonalFinance organic engagement.

Week 10

Post Download Nurture Per Product

Credit Builder: "Your credit score is X. Here's your 90 day improvement plan." Chat: "Cleo noticed you spent 40% more on food this week." UK: "Your Monzo round ups saved GBP 12 last month. Here's what Cleo would have done."

Week 11

Framework Documentation

Build the 4 week product validation sprint template. Document everything: targeting, creative, landing page, onboarding, metrics, go/no-go criteria. This becomes the playbook your Growth Marketing Lead inherits.

Week 12

Playbook Handoff

Hand the documented playbook to the Growth Marketing Lead. Launch the next wave of diversification products using the proven framework. First 90 day review: diversification CPA vs EWA CPA, UK acquisition rate, diversification revenue as percentage of total.

UK Re-Entry: Three Positioning Angles for Cleo

Angle 1: "Cleo + Your Bank" (Recommended)

Target: Monzo, Starling, and Revolut users (9M+ combined UK users)

Message: "Keep your bank. Add the AI. Cleo sits on top of your existing account and coaches you on what to do with your money."

Channel: Instagram UK, Reddit r/UKPersonalFinance

Why it works: Removes the "switch" objection. Positions Cleo as complementary, not competitive. Every Monzo user is a potential Cleo user.

Angle 2: "The Cost of Living Coach"

Target: 25 to 35 year olds struggling with rising costs

Message: "Your rent went up. Your bills went up. Your salary didn't. Cleo shows you exactly where to cut without giving up the things you love."

Channel: TikTok UK, YouTube financial education

Why it works: Taps into the UK cost of living crisis. Emotional trigger. Positions Cleo as a helper, not another subscription drain.

Angle 3: "Budgeting That Doesn't Feel Like Budgeting"

Target: 18 to 25 year olds who tried Plum, Emma, or Money Dashboard and bounced

Message: "Other budgeting apps lecture you. Cleo roasts you. 85% of users feel better about their finances within a month."

Channel: Instagram Reels, TikTok

Why it works: Personality is Cleo's moat. Plum is functional but personality free. Let the character sell the product.

Test recommendation: Run Angle 1 and Angle 3 head to head for 4 weeks. Angle 1 has the largest addressable market. Angle 3 has the strongest emotional hook. Winner gets scaled. Loser gets killed.

How Cleo Builds a Diversification Engine That Compounds

Three loops. Each one feeds the others. The longer they run, the stronger they get.

Loop 1: Product Launch to Channel Intelligence

Every diversification product launch generates data. Credit Builder's first 4 week sprint tells you which channels drive sign-ups, which messaging converts, which audiences activate.
